RESEARCH PROJECT SCOPE

Background Literature

In the early days of retailing all products had to be fetched by an assistant from shelves on one side of a counter while the customers stood on the other side and pointed to what they wanted. Also, many foods did not come in the individually wrapped consumer-size packages taken for granted today, so an assistant had to measure out the precise amount desired by the consumer. These practices were obviously labor-intensive and therefore quite expensive. The shopping process was slow, as the number of clerks employed in the store limited the number of customers who could be attended at one time. Therefore, the study aimed to provide a smart trolley for a supermarket which helps the customers to get benefit through the system. Techniques such as image processing, loyalty customer program, self-checkout, recommendations and voice assistant are used in order to enhance the performance of a smart trolley.

Research Problem

In supermarkets, every time customer has to put item one by one in the trolley and the total price. If the budget allows then go to billing counter where mall staff member does the scanning of each product then further move to the billing process. So, each customer has to wait in queue for his/her turn which takes too much of time. It is a troublesome process which resulting in a heavy crowd at the counters. If a person from outside saw people are waiting in a queue to pay bills, that particular person does not show eager to go to purchase. Supermarket customers find it difficult to choose products from a large variety of products. Access to products with common attributes prevents customers from searching in confusion or wasting time. Main Objectives

Developing a smart trolley for the enhancement of supermarket for the benefit of the customers.

Specific Objectives

  • Achieving faster billing system and also helps buyer to know the bill details in advance so that they can plan accordingly in affordable prices.
  • Providing support for the customers while purchasing by giving details about present day offers/details through voice assistance.
  • Recommending personalized items to customers to prevent customers from confusion of choosing products.
  • Allowing access to smart trolley using loyalty card.
  • Showing location of products.

MILESTONES

Project Proposal Presentation

The Project Proposal Presentation contributes to 6% of the final grade. Each team is allocated with 40 mins of presentation time. The interview panel consists of 3 experts who are relevant in the field in the system that is proposed. In this assessment overview of the domain, novelty of the system, aim of the study, methodology and budget should be provided in detail.

Project Proposal Presentation 1

The progress presentation- 1 contributes to 15% of the final grade. Each team is allocated with 30 mins of presentation time. The basic functionalities of each member in a team should able to show the 50% completion of the overall system.

Project Proposal Presentation 2

The progress presentation- II contributes to 18% of the final grade. Each team is allocated with 35 mins of presentation time. The basic functionalities of each member in a team should able to show the 90% completion of the overall system and the sub components should be integrated.
